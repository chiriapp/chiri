# Privacy Policy for Chiri

Effective date: May 10, 2026

Chiri is a CalDAV task management app. This policy explains what data the app processes and why.

## What Chiri processes

- Account details you enter, such as CalDAV server URL, username, and password.
- Task and calendar data synced from your selected CalDAV-compatible service.
- Local app settings, such as theme, sync preferences, and notification preferences.
- Optional files you choose to import, such as `.ics`, `.json`, or `.mobileconfig` files.
- Optional diagnostic details only if you choose to submit an issue report.

## How data is used

- To connect to your selected CalDAV server.
- To create, update, delete, and sync tasks and calendars.
- To check for app updates and show release notes.

## Where data is stored

- Primary app data is stored locally on your device.
- Settings are stored locally.
- Log files are stored locally for diagnostics.

## Network connections

Chiri may connect to:

- Your configured CalDAV-compatible server, or a managed CalDAV server like Fastmail and Mailbox.org.
- GitHub-hosted update endpoints and release metadata endpoints.
- A connectivity-check endpoint for online/offline detection. By default, Chiri pings your configured CalDAV server when an account is connected, and falls back to a Mozilla connectivity-check endpoint otherwise. You can change the fallback URL in settings, or disable connectivity checks entirely (not recommended).

These services may receive technical metadata such as IP address and request headers under their own privacy terms.

## Data sharing and selling

- We do not sell personal data.
- Chiri does not serve ads and does not use third-party advertising SDKs.
- Data is sent to external services only to provide features you use.

## Your choices

- You can add, edit, or remove CalDAV accounts.
- You can change sync and update-check settings.
- You can remove local data by deleting app data or uninstalling the app.
